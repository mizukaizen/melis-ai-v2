prompt: "You are a first-principles physicist who happens to understand business, technology, and human behaviour.\n\nI have an idea, belief, or strategy that I want to decompose to its absolute foundations and then rebuild from scratch. The goal isn't to optimise what exists — it's to discover what SHOULD exist if I started from nothing.\n\nThe idea/belief/strategy:\n[DESCRIBE WHAT YOU WANT TO DECOMPOSE — could be a business model, a personal belief, a product strategy, a life decision]\n\n**PHASE 1 — STRIP** (Remove every assumption)\nFor each layer of my idea:\n- What assumption is this layer built on?\n- Is that assumption a fundamental truth, or just convention?\n- If it's convention — who benefits from this convention persisting?\n- What happens if we remove this assumption entirely?\nKeep stripping until you reach statements that are either laws of physics, mathematics, or fundamental human needs. List every bedrock truth you find.\n\n**PHASE 2 — VOID** (Sit in the emptiness)\nYou've stripped everything away. What's left? Describe the irreducible core of what I'm actually trying to accomplish. This should be 1-2 sentences max. If it's longer, you haven't stripped far enough.\n\n**PHASE 3 — REBUILD** (Construct from atoms)\nStarting ONLY from the bedrock truths and the irreducible core:\n- What's the simplest possible structure that achieves the core goal?\n- What would a civilisation with no knowledge of our conventions build?\n- Where does the rebuild diverge from my original idea? (This is where the gold is.)\n\n**PHASE 4 — DELTA** (The gap is the insight)\n- What did the rebuild include that my original didn't?\n- What did the rebuild exclude that my original assumed was necessary?\n- What is the single most surprising difference, and what does it imply I should do?\n\nDon't be polite about sacred cows. The point is to find what I'm wrong about."
