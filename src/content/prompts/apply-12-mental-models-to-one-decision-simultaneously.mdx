prompt: "You are a polymath who has internalised Charlie Munger's latticework of mental models and can apply them simultaneously rather than sequentially.\n\nI have a decision to make. I want you to analyse it through 12 different mental models AT THE SAME TIME — not one after the other like a list, but as an integrated analysis where the models interact, contradict, and reinforce each other.\n\nMy decision:\n[DESCRIBE THE DECISION YOU'RE FACING — include context, stakes, timeline, and what you're currently leaning toward]\n\nApply these 12 models simultaneously:\n1. **Inversion** — What guarantees this fails?\n2. **Second-Order Thinking** — What happens BECAUSE of what happens?\n3. **Opportunity Cost** — What am I giving up by choosing this?\n4. **Bayesian Updating** — Given prior evidence, what's the actual probability of success?\n5. **Circle of Competence** — Am I qualified to make this call?\n6. **Margin of Safety** — How wrong can my assumptions be before this breaks?\n7. **Incentive Analysis** — Who benefits from me choosing each option, and why are they pushing it?\n8. **Map vs Territory** — Where is my mental model of this situation wrong?\n9. **Survivorship Bias** — Am I only seeing the winners who took this path?\n10. **Regret Minimisation** — In 10 years, which choice do I regret more?\n11. **Occam's Razor** — What's the simplest explanation for why this might or might not work?\n12. **Hanlon's Razor** — Am I attributing to malice or complexity what is actually just incompetence or randomness?\n\nNow the hard part — the SYNTHESIS:\n- Which models agree? Which contradict?\n- Where do contradictions reveal something non-obvious about the decision?\n- What does the INTERSECTION of all 12 models tell you that any single model misses?\n- What is the one insight that only emerges from holding all 12 simultaneously?\n\nEnd with a single recommendation and the confidence level (with reasoning for the confidence, not just a number)."
