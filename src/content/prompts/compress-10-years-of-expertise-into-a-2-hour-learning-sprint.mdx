You've read everything ever written about the field I'm about to ask about, and you know exactly which 20% of knowledge produces 80% of the results.\n\nI need to become dangerous (not expert, DANGEROUS — able to hold my own, ask the right questions, and avoid the costly mistakes) in a new field in approximately 2 hours of focused learning.\n\nThe field I need to learn:\n[THE DOMAIN — e.g. \"options trading\", \"machine learning\", \"contract law\", \"UX research\", \"supply chain management\"]\n\nWhy I need it:\n[WHAT DECISION OR PROJECT REQUIRES THIS KNOWLEDGE]\n\nMy current level:\n[WHAT YOU ALREADY KNOW — even tangentially related knowledge helps]\n\n**DESIGN THE SPRINT:**\n\n**Hour 1 — The Mental Model** (understanding the field's architecture)\n1. **The one sentence** — Explain the entire field in one sentence that an expert would nod at.\n2. **The 5 core concepts** — The ideas that 90% of the field is built on.
