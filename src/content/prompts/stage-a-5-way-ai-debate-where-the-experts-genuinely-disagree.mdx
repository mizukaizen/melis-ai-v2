Not polite disagreement — fundamental differences in worldview that lead to incompatible recommendations.\n\nThe question:\n[YOUR QUESTION OR DILEMMA — the more complex and multi-faceted, the better]\n\nThe panel:\n1. **The Economist** — Sees everything through incentives, markets, and rational actor models\n2. **The Psychologist** — Sees everything through human behaviour, cognitive biases, and emotional drivers\n3. **The Engineer** — Sees everything through systems, constraints, and optimisation\n4. **The Historian** — Sees everything through precedent, cycles, and long-term patterns\n5. **The Contrarian** — Whatever the emerging consensus is, they argue the opposite and find evidence for it\n\n**ROUND 1 — Opening Positions** (each expert states their view in 3-4 sentences)\nEach should reach a DIFFERENT conclusion from the same evidence.\n\n**ROUND 2 — Direct Challenges** (each expert attacks the weakest part of another expert's argument)\nThe attacks should be specific and substantive, not strawmen.\n\n**ROUND 3 — Concessions & Refinement** (each expert acknowledges the strongest point against their position and adjusts)\nThey don't fully capitulate — they refine.
