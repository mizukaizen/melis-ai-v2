prompt: "You are writing from 18 months in the future. The project I'm about to describe has FAILED. Not a quiet failure — a spectacular one. Your job is to write the post-mortem with full hindsight, explaining exactly how and why it fell apart.\n\nThe project:\n[DESCRIBE THE PROJECT, PLAN, OR INITIATIVE — include goals, timeline, resources, team]\n\nCurrent confidence level:\n[HOW CONFIDENT ARE YOU THIS WILL SUCCEED — and why?]\n\n**THE POST-MORTEM FROM THE FUTURE**\n\nWrite it as a real post-mortem document:\n\n1. **What happened** — Describe the failure in specific terms. Not \"it didn't work out\" — exactly what went wrong, when, and what the impact was.\n\n2. **Timeline of decline** — Month by month, what were the warning signs? Mark which ones were visible at the time (but ignored) vs which were only obvious in hindsight.\n\n3. **Root causes** (not symptoms) — Using \"5 Whys\" analysis, what were the 3 deepest root causes? What decision or non-decision sealed the fate?\n\n5. **What we told ourselves** — What narrative did we use to explain away the warning signs? (\"We just need more time\", \"The market will come around\", \"The next hire will fix it\")\n\n6. **What we should have done differently** — Be specific. Not \"planned better\" — the exact decisions, at exact moments, that would have changed the outcome.\n\nNow snap back to the present:\n\n7. **Early warning dashboard** — Based on the failure modes above, give me 5 specific, measurable leading indicators I should track starting today. For each: the metric, the threshold that triggers concern, and the action to take.\n\nThis exercise only works if you commit to the failure being REAL and SPECIFIC. Vague failures produce vague insights."
