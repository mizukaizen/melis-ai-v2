<p>The frame: two-way doors are reversible (decide fast, ship, learn). One-way doors are not (slow down, deliberate). Most decisions are two-way doors disguised as one-way doors.</p>

<p>The reversal: most operators I work with treat <em>everything</em> as a one-way door — and the result is paralysis. Or: they treat <em>everything</em> as two-way — and the result is debris.</p>
