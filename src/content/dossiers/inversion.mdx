<p>Jacobi was a 19th-century German mathematician who told his students to attack hard problems by flipping them backwards. Munger borrowed the principle and applied it to investment, business, and life — treating inversion not as a clever heuristic but as the default first move whenever a hard decision turned up.</p>

<p>The mechanic's dead simple. Standard thinking: "How do we build a great company?" Inverted: "What would reliably destroy a company?" The inverted list is almost always shorter, clearer, and easier to act on. You don't need to know the optimal move; you just need to avoid the catastrophic ones. Most people skip this half and pay for it later.</p>
