<p>Jacobi was a 19th-century German mathematician who advised his students to attack hard problems by reversing them. Munger borrowed the principle and applied it to investment, business and life. He treated inversion not as a clever heuristic but as the default first move when facing a hard decision.</p>

<p>The mechanic is simple. Standard thinking: "How do we build a great company?" Inverted: "What would reliably destroy a company?" The inverted list is usually shorter, clearer and easier to act on. The decision-maker does not need to know the optimal move; they only need to avoid the catastrophic ones.</p>
