<p>Wallace delivered the speech on 21 May 2005 to a graduating class of liberal arts students. He was forty-three. He took his own life three years later. The talk was published as a small book in 2009 and has circulated since as one of the most-cited pieces of secular ethical writing of the century.</p>

<p>The address is built around a single parable about fish who do not know what water is. Its argument is that adult life — the long stretches between Big Events — is mostly default-setting consciousness, and that the discipline of choosing what to pay attention to is the only freedom worth the name.</p>
