<p>Taleb published Antifragile in 2012, the third in his Incerto sequence after Fooled by Randomness and The Black Swan. The earlier books named the problem — the world is more uncertain than the models that price it. Antifragile is the constructive answer. If shocks cannot be predicted, things can at least be built to respond well to them.</p>

<p>The conceptual move is the trio: fragile, robust, antifragile. The fragile system breaks under stress (the wine glass). The robust system endures it (the rock). The antifragile system improves from it (the immune system, the muscle, the start-up that survives its first near-death and emerges leaner). The trio sounds obvious once stated. The work is noticing how many things assumed to be robust are actually fragile.</p>

<p>The operational core is the barbell. Most exposure should be hyper-conservative — cash, indexed savings, sleep, the boring things. A small allocation should be hyper-aggressive — convex bets where the downside is capped and the upside is open-ended. The middle — the "diversified medium-risk portfolio" — is the worst place to sit, because it is fragile to the events the model does not contain.</p>
