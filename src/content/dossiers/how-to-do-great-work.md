Graham published the essay in July 2023, twenty years after he started Y Combinator and four years after he stopped running it. By his own account, the essay is the book he had been meaning to write — distilled into a single long-form web page rather than a hardback. The runtime is roughly ninety minutes. Most readers read it in fragments over several months.

The essay's argument is unsystematic by design. Graham draws on his own work as a programmer, painter, essayist, and investor, but the substance is mostly examples — scientists, writers, founders — and the patterns he observed sitting across thousands of them. The patterns are not new; they are the same patterns that the older biographical literature on creative work has been describing for centuries. What the essay does well is state them in modern operational language for an audience that no longer reads the older sources.

The essay is most useful read at the wrong moment for it. Twenty-year-olds find it inspirational; forty-year-olds find it diagnostic. Graham's central instruction — that curiosity, not strategy, is the engine of the work that matters — reads as obvious until the reader notices how many of their own choices have been made on strategic rather than curious grounds.
