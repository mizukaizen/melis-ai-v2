<p>Chesterton offered the parable in an essay defending tradition against impatient reformers. Two men walk along a road and find a fence across it. The reformer wants to clear it away, seeing no purpose. The conservative refuses — unless and until the reformer can explain why the fence was built in the first place. Only then is demolition permitted.</p>

<p>The deeper point is not conservatism but humility about hidden function. Every fence, rule, ritual or norm exists because someone, at some point, had a reason. The reason may now be obsolete. Finding it is the precondition for safely removing the fence — not an obstacle to reform but its foundation.</p>
