<p>Clear assembled the book over a decade of newsletter writing, distilling behavioural research into a single operational frame: habits are compound interest applied to behaviour. A one per cent improvement, sustained, becomes a thirty-seven-fold improvement over a year. The maths echoes the maths Buffett applied to capital.</p>

<p>The mechanical core is the habit loop — cue, craving, response, reward — and four laws to engineer each step: make it obvious, attractive, easy, satisfying. The frame is not original. The discipline of writing each chapter as a single small lever the reader can pull tomorrow morning is.</p>

<p>The deeper move sits underneath the tactics. Clear argues that every action is a vote for the kind of person someone is becoming. The goal is not to read a book; it is to become a reader. The shift from outcome-thinking to identity-thinking is the part that survives once the chapter highlights fade.</p>
