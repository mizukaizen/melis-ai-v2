<p>The photograph was taken on 14 February 1990 by Voyager 1, looking back from beyond Neptune. Earth occupies less than a pixel — a pale blue dot. Sagan asked NASA to turn the camera around to take it, against the resistance of engineers who saw no scientific value.</p>

<p>The four-minute reading of the accompanying speech is the closest thing modern secular culture has to a sacred text. It is read at funerals, at graduations, at moments where someone needs a deliberate adjustment of scale.</p>
