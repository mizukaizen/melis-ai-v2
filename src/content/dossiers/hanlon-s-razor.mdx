<p>The line first appeared in a 1980 joke book of pseudo-Murphy laws. It has the ring of a much older proverb because it captures a pattern people have always known. The wording is provocative — "stupidity" is too strong — but the underlying observation holds.</p>

<p>The useful modern translation: most things that look like deliberate hostility are actually incompetence, fatigue, or systems failure. A client did not ignore an email out of contempt; they have three hundred unread. A team did not undermine the plan; nobody told them. A vendor did not lie; the right hand never spoke to the left hand.</p>
